Cheetah

A long-legged, swift-moving cat (Acinonyx jubatus) about the size of a small leopard with a yellowish to tan coat covered with numerous round to oval black spots and blunt claws that only partially retract and having a current range restricted to Africa and isolated parts of Iran

MERRIAM WEBSTER - Cheetah

What They Eat

Cheetahs are carnivores whose typical prey are small- to medium-size animals, such as:

  • Gazelles
  • Hares
  • Wildebeest (Young)
  • Warthogs
  • Birds

Where They Live

Cheetahs are found across Africa, primarily in northern Africa; the Sahel (the transition region between the Sahara to the north and the Sudanian Savanna to the south); and they are scattered across eastern and southern Africa, according to the Smithsonian. A small population lives in Iran, where they are critically endangered.
